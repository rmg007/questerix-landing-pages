# How to Write a Good Math Landing Page

Discover the key elements that make math curriculum landing pages effective at engaging educators and driving adoption.

![Hero image](/assets/generated/math-landing-hero.webp)

## Understanding Your Audience

Math landing pages typically target:
- **School administrators** focused on outcomes
- **Math teachers** concerned with practicality
- **Curriculum directors** looking for alignment
- **Parents** wanting better results for their children

## Essential Components

### 1. Clear Value Proposition
Your headline must immediately communicate:
- **What problem** you solve
- **For whom** you solve it
- **Why your solution** is different

Example: "Help Every Student Master Math With Adaptive Learning That Adjusts to Individual Needs"

### 2. Social Proof
Include:
- **Testimonials** from math teachers
- **Case studies** with measurable results
- **School logos** where you've succeeded
- **Statistics** showing improvement

### 3. Feature-Benefit Mapping
Don't just list features—connect them to outcomes:

| Feature | Benefit |
|---------|---------|
| Adaptive algorithms | Personalized learning paths |
| Real-time progress | Immediate intervention opportunities |
| Gamified practice | Increased student engagement |

## Addressing Math-Specific Concerns

### Common Objections:
- "Our teachers are already overwhelmed"
- "We don't have time for new technology"
- "Will this align with our standards?"
- "What about students who hate math?"

### Proactive Solutions:
- **Minimal implementation time**
- **Seamless LMS integration**
- **Standards alignment documentation**
- **Engagement-focused design**

## Visual Elements That Matter

### For Math Content:
- **Clean typography** for equations
- **Interactive demos** of problems
- **Progress visualizations**
- **Success story videos**

### Color Psychology:
- **Blue**: Trust and reliability
- **Green**: Growth and success
- **Orange**: Energy and engagement
- **Purple**: Innovation and creativity

## Call-to-Action Strategy

### Primary CTAs:
- **Request a demo** (high-intent users)
- **Start free trial** (hands-on evaluation)
- **Download sample** (low-commitment engagement)

### Secondary CTAs:
- **View case studies**
- **Check alignment**
- **Watch overview video**

## Mobile Optimization Considerations

Math content on mobile needs:
- **Readable equations** without zooming
- **Touch-friendly interfaces**
- **Fast loading times**
- **Offline capability** for homework

## Measuring Success

Track these metrics:
- **Conversion rate** by traffic source
- **Demo request quality**
- **Trial-to-paid conversion**
- **Time on page** by section

## A/B Testing Priorities

Test these elements first:
1. **Headline variations**
2. **Primary CTA text**
3. **Social proof placement**
4. **Feature ordering**

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

❌ **Too much technical jargon**
❌ **Ignoring teacher concerns**
❌ **No clear next steps**
❌ **Outdated success metrics**
❌ **Mobile-unfriendly design**

## Writing Tips for Math Content

- **Use simple language** for complex concepts
- **Show, don't just tell** with examples
- **Address pain points** directly
- **Provide clear evidence** of effectiveness

## Conclusion

Great math landing pages balance technical credibility with emotional appeal. They speak to the head (data, results) and heart (student success, teacher empowerment) to create compelling reasons to explore your solution further.

Ready to create landing pages that convert? Focus on clarity, credibility, and addressing the specific concerns of math educators.
