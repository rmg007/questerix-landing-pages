# Mastery-Based Progression Explained: Moving Beyond Traditional Grading

Learn how mastery-based progression creates a learning environment where every student can succeed, focusing on understanding over arbitrary deadlines.

![Hero image](/assets/generated/mastery-progression-hero.webp)

## What is Mastery-Based Progression?

Mastery-based progression is an educational approach where students advance through curriculum only after demonstrating mastery of specific concepts and skills. Unlike traditional systems that move students forward based on time spent, mastery learning ensures true understanding before progression.

## The Problem with Traditional Grading

Traditional grading systems often:
- **Push students forward** regardless of understanding
- **Create knowledge gaps** that compound over time
- **Focus on completion** rather than comprehension
- **Leave struggling students** behind without support

## How Mastery Learning Works

### Core Principles:
1. **Clear learning objectives** for each concept
2. **Multiple assessment opportunities** to demonstrate mastery
3. **Personalized pacing** based on individual needs
4. **Targeted interventions** when students struggle
5. **Advancement only after mastery** is demonstrated

### The Learning Cycle:
1. **Learn** the concept through instruction
2. **Practice** with guided exercises
3. **Assess** understanding through low-stakes quizzes
4. **Remediate** if mastery isn't achieved
5. **Advance** only when ready

## Benefits for Students

### For Advanced Students:
- **Move faster** through mastered concepts
- **Explore deeper** applications of knowledge
- **Stay engaged** with appropriate challenges
- **Build confidence** through continuous success

### For Struggling Students:
- **Get the time** needed to understand
- **Receive targeted support** exactly when needed
- **Build confidence** through small wins
- **Avoid falling behind** permanently

## Implementation Challenges

Without proper tools, mastery learning can be:
- **Time-intensive** for teachers
- **Difficult to track** at scale
- **Complex to manage** individual progress
- **Hard to align** with traditional schedules
