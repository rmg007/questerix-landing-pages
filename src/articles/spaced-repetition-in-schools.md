# Spaced Repetition in Schools: The Science of Long-Term Learning

Discover how the cognitive science behind spaced repetition can help students retain information longer and study more effectively.

## What is Spaced Repetition?

Spaced repetition is a learning technique that involves reviewing information at increasing intervals over time. Instead of cramming, students revisit material just before they're about to forget it, strengthening neural pathways and moving knowledge to long-term memory.

## The Science Behind It

Research in cognitive psychology has consistently shown that spacing out study sessions leads to better retention than massed practice. This phenomenon, known as the "spacing effect," was first documented by Hermann Ebbinghaus in the 1880s and has been validated by countless modern studies.

### Key Benefits:
- **40% better retention** compared to cramming
- **Reduced study time** by reviewing only what needs reinforcement
- **Increased confidence** as students see their progress
- **Lower cognitive load** during learning sessions

Implementing spaced repetition doesn't require overhauling your entire curriculum. Start with:

1. **Identify key concepts** that benefit from memorization
2. **Create or import** question sets

4. **Monitor progress** and adjust as needed
